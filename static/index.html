      Disease Library

      Comprehensive reference guide for all 5 cassava leaf pathologies detected by the diagnostic model.

      5 Classified Diseases

      CassavaGuard detects and classifies five distinct cassava leaf conditions — from critical pathologies to healthy specimens — enabling rapid field diagnosis and intervention.

      CBB
      Cassava Bacterial Blight
      CBB Critical
      A systemic vascular disease caused by Xanthomonas axonopodis pv. manihotis. Colonizes vascular tissue, causes angular leaf spots, stem dieback, and gum exudates. Spreads rapidly through rain splash and contaminated tools.
      Angular leaf spotsStem diebackGum exudateVascular browning
      Max Yield Loss
      100%
      CBSD
      Brown Streak Disease
      CBSD High Risk
      Caused by Cassava Brown Streak Viruses transmitted by whiteflies. Produces vein banding on leaves and severe necrotic pitting in roots, rendering tubers entirely unmarketable. A major threat in East Africa.
      Vein bandingRoot necrosisBrown stem streaksRoot pitting
      Max Yield Loss
      70%
      CGM
      Green Mottle
      CGM Moderate
      Caused by Cassava Green Mottle Virus. Produces chlorotic mottling patterns that reduce photosynthetic efficiency. Less economically devastating than CMD or CBSD, but still causes measurable yield loss in affected crops.
      Chlorotic mottlingLeaf distortionMild stunting
      Max Yield Loss
      30%
      HLT
      Healthy
      HLT No Risk
      No visible signs of disease or pest damage. Leaves are uniformly deep green, fully expanded without curling, deformity or discolouration. Confirmation of optimal growing conditions.
      Uniform deep greenNo deformationFull expansion
      Yield Impact
      None
      CMD
      Cassava Mosaic Disease
      CMD Critical
      The most devastating cassava disease in Africa. Caused by cassava mosaic geminiviruses (CMGs), transmitted by the silverleaf whitefly. Characteristic mosaic and chlorotic leaf patterns; severely stunted growth and up to 95% yield loss in severe outbreaks.
      Mosaic patternsLeaf curlingChlorosisSevere stunting
      Max Yield Loss
      95%

      About CassavaGuard

      An AI-powered diagnostic platform built on transfer learning to assist agricultural researchers and field workers in identifying cassava leaf diseases rapidly, accurately, and at scale.

      ResNet50 Backbone

      Pretrained ImageNet ResNet50 backbone with custom BatchNorm + Linear FC head, fine-tuned for precise 5-class cassava pathology classification.

      Two-Phase Training

      Phase 1: frozen backbone trains the FC classification head. Phase 2: fine-tunes the final residual block with cosine annealing for optimal convergence.

      Actionable Insights

      Beyond raw classification — each diagnosis includes disease descriptions, visible symptoms, severity rating, and recommended agricultural interventions.
